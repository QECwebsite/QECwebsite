Competition Categories

Select the challenge that pushes your strengths and helps you develop new ones.

Team size: 2
Eligible years: All years

Communication

Explain complex engineering or science topics in accessible language that sparks curiosity and showcases engineering impact.

Team size: 2
Eligible years: All years

Debate

Build persuasive arguments and deliver well-reasoned viewpoints on surprise resolutions announced the day of competition.

Team size: 3-4
Eligible years: All years

Consulting

Deliver strategic recommendations for large-scale engineering problems, balancing technical and socio-economic implications.

Team size: 2
Eligible years: All years

Bio-Engineering

Develop bio-engineering solutions for healthcare, agriculture, or sustainability challenges presented in the prompt.

Team size: 3-4
Eligible years: All years

Innovation

Create or enhance market-ready products or services backed by strong engineering feasibility and business rationale.

Team size: 2
Eligible years: All years

Re-Engineering

Redesign existing products or processes to improve function or adapt them for new applications with market-ready outcomes.

Team size: 3-4
Eligible years: Junior: 1st-2nd, Senior: 3rd+

Design (Junior & Senior)

Build functional prototypes for real-world challenges with expectations scaled for junior and senior competitors.

Team size: 3-4
Eligible years: All years

Programming

Competitors aim to develop industry-quality software to solve a given engineering problem. Students will use their software development, technical writing, as well as project management skills to create a creative solution

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before you register.

What is the Queen's Engineering Competition?

QEC brings together students to solve real-world engineering challenges while developing professional skills and connecting with industry leaders.

Who can participate?

Registration is open to all engineering students at Queen's University. Form a team that complements your skill sets and take on the challenge.

How do I register?

Select your competition stream, assemble your teammates, and complete the registration form. You'll receive follow-up details once your submission is received.

Ready to Compete?

Registration is open to all engineering students at Queen's. Form a team and take on the challenge.
